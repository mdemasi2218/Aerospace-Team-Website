Icarus (2022-2023)

Introducing the ICARUS plane: a testament to our team's unwavering dedication and relentless pursuit of innovation. Despite its tragic end, this marvel of engineering showcased our expertise. A fully wooden design, each detachable component expertly crafted to fit inside a 62-inch cubed shipping box. With unprecedented power of up to 42 amps, it marked our most ambitious creation. Though we faced adversity, we emerged stronger, armed with invaluable knowledge and the determination to soar higher.

pHATbASS (2021-2022)

Designed for the 2021-2022 DBF competition, this plane embodies the ongoing pandemic with delivery of syringes along with the lastest design related to the Hercules C130. Our team made a huge comeback after Covid, marking this as our first aircraft taken to competition in school history where we placed 48th out of 127 schools worldwide!

We would like to sincerely thank our donors and contributors for their continued generous support. In particular, we would like to thank Northrop Grumman, Boeing, the P.C. Rossin College of Engineering and Applied Sciences, and all of our supporters from the Lehigh Crowdfunding Campaign! Your contributions helped make our dream come true!

COV-1 (2020-2021)

The COV-1, gets its name from the COVID-19 pandemic. This aircraft features a double boom, which must deploy and retract a sensor during mid-flight. Due to the multiple setbacks and restrictions we faced for the majority of the year, we were unable to complete a finished aircraft for the 2020-2021 DBF competition. However, we completed a flying prototype that was used for learning and flight testing for future years!

POD-2 (2019-2020)

The POD-2 (Plane of Death) gets its name from its powerful motor. This is a delta wing aircraft, which we designed to fly at high speeds while carrying a certain number of passengers for the 2019-2020 DBF competition. Previous prototypes have been tested and optimized before creating the final aircraft as depicted in the picture. Overall, this has been one of our most successful years in terms of aircraft design and flight testing, we even had a dogfight between two of our prototypes!

CUP-10 (2018-2019)

The CUP-10 (Cruel and Unusual Punishment) gets its name from the difficult design challenges we faced while making this aircraft. For the 2018-2019 DBF competition, our aircraft had to be designed to rotate the wing back in order to fit inside a 3x3 ft. box with a 5ft wingspan. Also, it had to store and drop foam “bombs” during mid-flight. We spent most of our time in the design phase due to the challenges presented to us, and because of this, we were unable to have effective flight data for the competition.

BUB-Y (2017-2018)

The BUB-Y (Big Ugly Bird) gets its name from its blocky looks. The main objective for DBF competition during the 2017-2018 year was to create an aircraft that can carry as many passengers as possible. Our team developed a conventional style aircraft to complete this goal, and was able to get plenty of data from designing and flight testing. This was the earliest recorded year of our competition aircraft data.
